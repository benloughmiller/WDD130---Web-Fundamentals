Oranges are a delicious fruit that are healthy and great sources of vitamin C. Oranges have a wide range of uses and some may surprise you! Oranges are praised for their sweet taste and the amount of vitamin C they contain. Vitamin C is a great immune system booster and has been linked to reduce the risk of illness, from common colds to cancer. If you would like to learn more about the uses and health benefits of oranges, navigate to our page on the uses of oranges.

UNITED STATES ORANGE CONSUMPTION

The United States Department of Agriculture (USDA) estimated in 2000 that the average American consumes 13-15 pounds of oranges per year, and up to 75 pounds of Orange Juice. It is no wonder that oranges are among the most grown fruits in America.

ORANGE GROWING IN AMERICA

Oranges grow best in subtropical environments, which is why the state of Florida alone accounts for more than 70% of all orange growing in America, followed by California and Texas. There are some varieties of oranges that grow well in colder environments. If you would like to learn more about growing oranges, navigate to our orange growing guide on this website.
